"Sky Fish," Artifact of Speculative Fiction, 2021

Artifact: Ancient Sculpture of a Mythical Flying Creature: Sky Fish (Bird)

In a world where humanity never made it onto land, and instead evolved into a species that dwells within the sea, birds are seen as mythical creatures. After attempts to venture out of the sea and onto land, humans learned that they cannot breathe above water since their gills only allow them to intake oxygen from water. As a result, expeditions onto land had halted until humanity found a way to breathe on land or survive long enough to explore past the coast. Those who had bravely ventured out of the sea, told tales of soaring creatures with strong, sharp beaks with the ability to swim through air, unlike humans and all other sea creatures. These stories led to the creation of visual representations of these creatures that many believed were fish that learned to swim in air, while humanity may not have collectively believed in these tall tales nor in the existence of these creatures. The visual portrayals of these creatures, referred to as sky fish, share similar visual qualities with the sea creatures that humans are so familiar with. This artifact is an ancient sculpture of the aforementioned sky fish created by famous artist Michaelangelfish during his childhood after Leonardo da Fishi’s sand paintings of the sky fish, estimated to have been created in 1482. The sculpture is constructed from a variety of ocean materials including sand, seaweed, and a small variety of sea shells. Eventually, when the gills of humans adapted to be able to intake oxygen from both air and water, humans became an amphibian species that can survive on land or in water. After this evolution, humans discovered an animal species similar to their mythical sky fish that they came to refer to as birds. While some humans believe that birds evolved from sky fish who evolved from fish, others believe that sky fish were figments of the ancient adventurers’ imaginations or exaggerations of the birds that humans see today. The artifact was found in Michaelangelfish’s coral reef studio amongst more of his more famous artworks by a group of ocean travelling art historians.
