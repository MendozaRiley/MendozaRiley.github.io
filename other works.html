"Don't Look"


"Don't Look," Acrylic Paint on Canvas, 9" x 12",2019.

This painting represents contradiction. While the text commands the viewer not to look, the image draws them in, leading to the question of whether they should continue to look or not.


"Troll"


"Troll," 9" x 12", 2020.

I transferred a group of images onto a sheet of acrylic glass using acrylic matte medium to create a collage that represented the different layers of Twitter's virtual environment as an online discussion platform.

"Capsule"


"Capsule," 2020.

This piece reflects the different layers to a person through their public and private identities. There's the version of oneself that people are comfortable showing, but there's also another side that one may not want others to see. This side may include qualities that we don't want others to see out of embarrassment and shame or fear. While the capsule showcases a lively, inviting exterior, there are many secrets hidden beneath, that are out of the viewer's reach.









"Once Upon a Time"


"Once Upon a Time," 2020.

This three-dimensional font reflects the inviting nature of fictional worlds that allow one to temporarily escape from reality. This piece also serves as a reminder that despite the realism and depth of well-written novels, the fantastical realms are only alive within the pages of the books.
