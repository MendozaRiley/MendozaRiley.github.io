"Don't Look," Online Experience, 2023 (In Progress)

This is my current progress for my final BFA project at San Jose State University. This project will be a browser based game that explores the theme of the conflicting relationship between the fear of being perceived and the desire to be seen and remembered.

Initial Ideas and Inspiration

This piece relates to my work so far not only through its visual relationship to some past drawings and paintings of mine, but also to its personal narrative qualities. For the visual components of the project, I'm drawing inspiration from the aesthetics of that doodle-like drawings the I create in my free time. These drawings feature an array of eyes and detailed patterns, designs, and imagery that I plan to utilize in my BFA project

Project Description:

The home page will be a digital version of an old painting of mine that shares the same title and theme. The player will be taken to the first level of the game by clicking on the center of the eyeball on the home page. The game will involve 13 levels where the player must search through the detailed, vibrantly colored doodles and click on the girl in black and white in order to advance. Each level will lead the viewer through a personal narrative of contradictory feelings and desires related to perception and attention that I often experience.

For the coding aspect of the game, I plan to use p5.js with a combination of images and sprites. The main navigation function for the piece would be scrolling, as the viewer searches through each level. Regarding the visual components of the game, each level will be filled with doodle-like, colorful images involving an array of eyes and detailed patterns and designs. Each level will reflect a different stage in life, involving their own set of related imagery and symbols, but all levels will be tied to eyes and the idea of ‘looking.’ While the imagery and textual components of the game will explicitly ward off the viewer, it will also implicitly tempt them to ‘look.’

In relation to the physical exhibition of the work, I envision the project being presented in a dimly lit space. The room does not need to be completely dark, but it would be best if the space was almost solely illuminated by the works present. In addition, a space shared with other vibrantly colored pieces would be ideal. I want the piece to be displayed near the middle of a wall, avoiding the corners of the room.
