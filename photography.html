Digital Photography


"Imagination"









"Imagination," Digital Photography, 2020.

This collection of photos portrays my interpretation of the word "imagination." Initially, I planned for the images to reflect the excitement, liveliness, and youthfulness associated with imagination. However, after photographing a few different scenes, I felt as if I had captured something else. I originally believed that an imaginative outlook involved seeing the world through bright, colorful lenses, but I realized that is not necessarily true. A person’s imagination can be dark as well. Sometimes an imaginative perspective paired with fear may cause someone to be afraid of something that isn’t even real. When I was a child, I was not necessarily as afraid of darkness as I was of what I thought may be lurking in it. Thus, there are numerous different aspects to a person’s imagination, and I tried to explore my own through these photos.


"Reality and Transformation"











"Reality and Transformation," Digital Photography, 2020.

This is a collection of photos that reflects the manipulation of reality inspired by dreams. I wanted to portray the surreal realm created by the random, confusing, and often meaningless nature of dreams through photography. This series consists of three main scenes. Each scene includes a photo that has not undergone as large of an amount of editing as the others which maintains the image’s attachment to reality. However, as the narratives progress, the concept of what is real becomes distorted. This transformation reveals the chaotic energy of dreams and the subconscious where the presence of so many layers of thought, memories, and identity begin to blur and intertwine.


"RIP to my Youth"


"RIP to my Youth," Digital Photography, 2020.

This collection of photos explores the concept of growing up and out of the habits and interests of one’s younger years. This narrative showcases the fear and inner conflict experienced when looking towards adulthood and total independence. While each individual’s fears and worries about their future may vary, most if not all feel the uncertainty of the future looming over them at one point or another in their lives. Inspired by my own feelings of existential questioning, I wanted to portray some of the nostalgia and anxiety felt when on the cusp of adulthood.
