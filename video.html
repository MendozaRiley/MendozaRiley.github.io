"Can't Talk Right Now, Feeding My Llama"

"Can't Talk Right Now, Feeding My Llama," Digital Video, 1 m 15 s, 2020

This is a five shot digital video. This piece portrays the random and surreal experiences of dreams that often do not make any sense.

Featuring: Gabe Mendoza


"It Was a Couple of Days Ago"

"It Was a Couple of Days Ago," Digital Video, 3 m 51 s, 2020

The topic of this piece is manipulative editing and its ability to blur the lines between what is real and fake. The narrative of a video is dependent on its portrayal through the editing. Thus, the person or group in control of the video creation has a lot of power. Since video is a convenient and efficient way to get information out to a large amount of people, it can be easy to take advantage of its storytelling power to further one's personal agendas rather than convey the truth.

Featuring: Gabe Mendoza and Lily Mendoza


"Ticking"


"Ticking," Digital Video, 6 m 14 s, December 2020

This video explores the interrelationship between light and darkness in people regarding how they deal with emotions of annoyance, anger, and aggravation. The narrative presents the seemingly comical interactions between two siblings who do not get along very well, as their disdain for each other grows. Instead of communicating their issues with one another, they bottle up their anger until they explode. The video concludes ambiguously, leaving the viewer alone with their own interpretation. This piece contains some violence.

Featuring: Gabe Mendoza and Connie Mendoza

"Meet Yin and Yang"

"Meet Yin and Yang," Augmented Reality, November 2020

This is an interactive AR work that portrays the intertwined relationship between light and darkness. The scene parallels the two complementary forces through a combination of illustrations, GIFs, and 3D objects. The presence of both concepts in the AR scene reflects the simultaneous existence of light and darkness in humanity and life itself. This piece features clips and animated illustrations of the two main characters from my collaboration video installation "YinYang" with Lily Mendoza.

"YinYang"

Note: Unmute Audio

"YinYang", Virtual Digital Video Installation, 3 m 43 s, 2020

I collaborated with Lily Mendoza for "YinYang." This piece is inspired by the complementary relationship between light and darkness. While the videos initially separate the light from the darkness through parallels and juxtaposition, the narrative concludes with the presence of both forces in both worlds and characters. Thus, the work reveals the interrelation of both concepts which are a part of every person and the basis of the complexity of human nature and life itself. This collection of four short films are meant to be projected simultaneously on the four walls of a room. We wanted to immerse the viewer in the two realms that we created, so they can experience the light and dark closely without escape.

Featuring: Leksi Lincoln, Lanie Lincoln, and Gabe Mendoza
